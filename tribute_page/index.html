The face of Radioactivity

Marie Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize. She's the only person to win a Nobel Prize in 2 sciences. Here's the timeline of her life:

  • November 7, 1867: Maria Sk≈Çodowska is born in Poland
  • 1891: Marie goes to Paris to study at Sorbonne
  • 1895: Marie marries Pierre Curie, which in turn ends ip with her changing her name
  • 1897: Marie's first daugther, Irene, is born
  • 1902: Marie is hired to do some experiments; while conducting them, she discovers pure radium
  • 1903: Marie and her husband, Pierre, are awarded the Nobel Prize in physics for their work with radioactivity
  • 1904: Marie is promoted to the chief assistant to her husband in his laboratory; Pierre begins teaching at Sorbonne
  • 1906: Marie's husband, Pierre, is killed in an accident, so she takes his place teaching at Sorbonne and is the first woman professor there
  • 1911: Marie wins her second Nobel Prize, this time in chemistry and on her own, for her continued work in radioactivity
  • 1914: During WWI, Marie uses her radioactive research materials to find bullets and shrapnel in wounded soldiers with the help of x-rays
  • 1922: Marie becomes a member of the French Academy of Medicine and devotes herself to using her knowledge to help people
  • 1929: Marie visits America and is given a gram of radium as a gift
  • 1932: Marie and her sister start a research facility to help fight cancer (which is still opened today - Marie Sklodowska Curie Oncology Center)
  • 1934: Marie Curie gets sick and dies shortly after
